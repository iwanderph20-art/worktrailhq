📅 Appointment Booking
Intake Scheduler · Setup & Integration Guide

Thanks for your purchase! This is a single file (scheduling.html) that runs in any browser — no install, no account, no subscription. Clients pick a service, see your real openings, and book. Here's how to set it up and put it to work.

1 · Add your business, services & hours

1
Open the app.

Double-click scheduling.html — it opens in your browser.

2
Open Settings.

Tap ⚙ (top-right). Enter your business name, page heading, an intro line, and upload your logo.

3
Add your services.

Under Services, give each one a name, a short description, its length in minutes, and an optional price. Add as many as you offer. Time slots are built from each service's length.

4
Set your weekly hours.

Turn on the days you take appointments and set open/close times for each. Times outside your hours are never offered.

2 · Fine-tune your booking rules

Under Booking rules you control exactly how times are offered:

  • Time-slot spacing — how far apart start times are offered (e.g. every 30 min).
  • Gap between appts — a buffer added after each booking for cleanup, travel, or notes.
  • Minimum notice — how soon someone can book (e.g. no bookings within 12 hours).
  • How far ahead — the furthest out clients can book (e.g. 30 days).

Under Days off / blocked dates, block specific dates for holidays or vacation — those days show no openings.

How slots work. For each open day, the app offers start times from your open hour until the last time a full service still fits before you close — and it automatically hides any time that overlaps an existing booking (plus your buffer). You can never be double-booked.

Option A · Use it at your front desk

Open the file on a tablet or reception computer to book walk-ins and phone calls yourself. Every booking is saved on that device — open Appointments to search, cancel (which reopens the slot), export a CSV, or print your day. Nothing is uploaded. This is the most private option.

Option B · Post it as a link

Put the file online and share the address anywhere — your "Book now" button, Google Business profile, email signature, or bio. Free ways to host it:

  • Netlify Drop — go to app.netlify.com/drop and drag scheduling.html in. You instantly get a public link.
  • Your existing web host — upload scheduling.html (e.g. yourbusiness.com/book.html).
  • GitHub Pages, Cloudflare Pages, Google Sites — any static hosting works.

Want just the booking flow, without the owner-only Appointments and ⚙ buttons? Add ?embed=1:

https://yourbusiness.com/book.html?embed=1

You can also force a look with ?theme=dark or ?theme=light.

Option C · Embed it in your website

Paste this where you want the booker to appear (most site builders have an "Embed" or "Custom HTML" block — Squarespace, Wix, WordPress, Webflow, GoDaddy, etc.):

<iframe
  src="https://yourbusiness.com/book.html?embed=1"
  style="width:100%;height:900px;border:0;"
  title="Book an appointment"></iframe>

Replace the src with wherever you hosted the file (Option B).

Receiving bookings from a posted / embedded page

When a client books on a posted or embedded page from their own phone, the booking is saved on their device — so to receive it, connect a free delivery URL once. Open ⚙ → Settings → "Send bookings to" and paste an endpoint. Every booking is then sent there automatically (hit Send test booking to confirm it arrives).

  • Email it to you — create a free endpoint at Web3Forms or Formspree, copy the URL, and paste it in.
  • Log to a spreadsheet — use a Google Sheet + Apps Script Web App URL so every booking lands in a row.
  • Into your CRM / calendar / SMS — paste a Zapier or Make.com webhook to route bookings anywhere.
Important — one shared list vs. separate devices. Bookings are stored in the browser they're made in. On a front-desk device that's perfect — every booking is in one place. If you post or embed the page so clients book from their own phones, those bookings won't appear in your front-desk list unless you connect a delivery URL — so always add one for a public booking page, and treat the email/sheet it feeds as your master schedule.
