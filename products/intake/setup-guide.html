⚖️ Client Intake
Law-Firm Intake Form · Setup & Integration Guide

Thanks for your purchase! Client Intake is a single file (intake.html) that runs in any browser — no install, no account. You can use it three ways: on an office device, as a link you post, or embedded in your website. Here's how to set up each.

1 · Brand it (2 minutes)

1
Open the app.

Double-click intake.html — it opens in your browser.

2
Customize.

Click ⚙ (top-right). Set your firm name, form heading, an intro line, and upload your logo. Tap Save.

3
Day or night.

Use the 🌙 / ☀️ button to pick a light or dark look. Your choice is remembered.

Option A · Use it on an office device (kiosk)

Perfect for a front-desk tablet or reception computer. Clients (or staff) fill in the form and tap Submit. Every inquiry is saved on that device — open Submissions to search, export a CSV, or print. Nothing is uploaded. This is the most private option.

Tip. On an iPad, open the file in Safari and choose Add to Home Screen for a clean full-screen form.

Option B · Post it as a link

Put the file online and share the address anywhere — your website "Contact us" button, Google Business profile, email signature, or social bio. Free ways to host it:

  • Netlify Drop — go to app.netlify.com/drop and drag intake.html in. You instantly get a public link.
  • Your existing web host — upload intake.html to your site (e.g. yourfirm.com/intake.html).
  • GitHub Pages, Cloudflare Pages, Google Sites — any static hosting works.

Want just the form, without the firm-only Submissions and ⚙ buttons? Add ?embed=1 to the link:

https://yourfirm.com/intake.html?embed=1

You can also force a look with ?theme=dark or ?theme=light.

Option C · Embed it in your website

Paste this where you want the form to appear (most site builders have an "Embed" or "Custom HTML" block — Squarespace, Wix, WordPress, Webflow, GoDaddy, etc.):

<iframe
  src="https://yourfirm.com/intake.html?embed=1"
  style="width:100%;height:1200px;border:0;"
  title="New Client Intake"></iframe>

Replace the src with wherever you hosted the file (Option B).

Receiving inquiries from a posted / embedded form

When a client fills out a posted or embedded form on their own phone or computer, the entry is saved on their device — so to receive it, connect a free delivery URL once. Open ⚙ → “Send submissions to” and paste an endpoint. Every submission is then sent there automatically (and you can hit Send test submission to confirm it arrives).

  • Email it to you — create a free form endpoint at Web3Forms or Formspree, copy the URL, and paste it in. Inquiries land in your inbox.
  • Log to a spreadsheet — use a Google Sheet + Apps Script Web App URL (same method as our other tools).
  • Into your CRM / anywhere — paste a Zapier or Make.com webhook to route inquiries to Clio, MyCase, Slack, SMS, or 6,000+ apps.
Which option do I need? Using the form on your own office device? No delivery URL needed — everything's in Submissions. Posting a link or embedding it for clients to fill remotely? Add a delivery URL so the inquiries reach you.
A note for your clients. The form reminds visitors that submitting it does not create an attorney-client relationship and asks them not to share highly sensitive details — standard, protective language for a law firm. Edit the intro text anytime under ⚙.
